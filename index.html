main instruments

guitar

British voiced amps are always a good start. Marshall, orange, Laney, hovercraft, although a lot of sludge bands used crates and Randall solid state amps, and I believe crowbar uses 5150's. There's really no rules to guitars, although the traditional way to go is a Gibson, I've seen people use telecasters no problem. Most doom bands use either fuzz or overdrive or a combination, high gain distortion pedals aren't really used that often because people would usually rather get that tube amp distortion. If you started your rig off with an orange, an sg/lp tuned down, and a super overdrive and/or a big muff you'd have a decent starting point for what you're looking for.

drum

Most of the real good sounding gear is either going to be impossible to find or super expensive but their are some good budget choices too. I highly suggest checking out a peavey Windsor if your on a budget. Super cheap and has a great stoner sound out of the box but can sound even better with some easy mods. I play through a sovtek MIG 100h and an emperor cab but the other dude I jam with has a Windsor thru a Marshall 1960 and it sounds great too.

bass

A lot of Kyuss/Qotsa sounds can be had running any sort of muff variant and using the neck pickup of a humbucker guitar. They also happened to use a lot of old Ampeg bass amps, though I know Josh uses a whole range of amps now. Down tuning is pretty important, and as far as effects fuzz and OD are the biggest ones, though I find an EQ pedal pushing all the frequencies to the max can also sound pretty awesome.
